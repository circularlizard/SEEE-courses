# **NNAS Navigator Awards: Comprehensive Online Lesson Outline**

## **üß≠ BRONZE LEVEL: Foundations of Navigation**

*Target Audience: 14-16 Year Olds (DofE/Scouts)*

*Focus: Path-based navigation and basic map literacy.*

### **Module 1: The Landscape of Maps**

**Lesson 1.1: The Spectrum of Maps** (6 mins)

* **Content:** Comparing tools. Why Google Maps is for driving; why Komoot/AllTrails is for inspiration; why OS 1:50k is for cycling; and why Harvey/OS 1:25k is for walking. Focus on 1:25k as the "Gold Standard" for detail.  
* **Imagery Needs:** 5-way split screen of the same hilltop across different map types.  
* **Video Note:** Quick "Map Reveal" showing the detail lost when zooming out on digital apps vs. paper.

**Lesson 1.2: Decoding the Symbols** (6 mins)

* **Content:** Categorizing symbols: Linear (Rights of Way), Area (Woods/Forests), Point (Trig points, ruins), and Water (Streams).  
* **Imagery Needs:** "Spot the Difference" graphic of dashed line styles. Photo of a real feature next to its symbol.

**Lesson 1.3: Scale & Space** (5 mins)

* **Content:** Visualizing 1:25,000. "4cm \= 1km." Using the blue 1km grid squares to estimate journey length.  
* **Imagery Needs:** Photo of 1km road next to a map snippet with a ruler overlay showing 4cm.

**Module 1 Quiz: The Map Decoder Challenge**

* **Question 1 (Matching):** Match 5 symbols to their real-world photo.  
* **Question 2 (Multiple Choice):** "8cm on a 1:25k map is how far on the ground?" (2km).  
* **Question 3 (Image Hotspot):** "Click on the symbol for a path you are legally allowed to ride a bike on."

### **Module 2: Tools & Position**

**Lesson 2.1: The Tool Spectrum (Digital vs. Paper)** (6 mins)

* **Content:** Why paper/compass for DofE? Discussing phone failure points (battery, cold, signal) vs. paper reliability.  
* **Imagery Needs:** "Reliability Scorecard" infographic.

**Lesson 2.2: The 4-Figure Box** (4 mins)

* **Content:** "Along the corridor, then up the stairs." Finding the 1km square neighborhood.  
* **Imagery Needs:** Animated map overlay showing fingers moving along Eastings then up Northings.

**Lesson 2.3: 6-Figure Precision** (6 mins)

* **Content:** Slicing the 1km box into 10ths. Pinpointing a specific gate or bridge.  
* **Imagery Needs:** High-res zoom of one grid square with a "roamer" scale overlay.

**Module 2 Quiz: Pinpoint Precision**

* **Question 1 (Image Answering):** "Identify the 4-figure grid reference for the square containing the Windmill."  
* **Question 2 (Ordering):** Order the steps: Easting \-\> 10ths across \-\> Northing \-\> 10ths up.

### **Module 3: The Earth has Wrinkles (Contours)**

**Lesson 3.1: Introducing the Lines** (5 mins)

* **Content:** 5m/10m intervals. Steep vs Flat spacing.  
* **Imagery Needs:** 3D model of a hill side-by-side with its 2D contour representation.  
* **Video Note:** The "Potato Slice" demonstration video.

**Lesson 3.2: Spurs, Valleys, and Summits** (6 mins)

* **Content:** The "V" shape rule. Identifying basic UK landforms.  
* **Imagery Needs:** Photos of real landforms with contours drawn over the top in orange.

**Module 3 Quiz: The 3D Vision Test**

* **Question 1 (Visual Matching):** Match patterns (V-up, V-down, Circle) to names (Valley, Spur, Summit).

### **Module 4: The Compass Tool**

**Lesson 4.1: Choosing Your Compass** (5 mins)

* **Content:** Button vs Lensatic vs Digital vs Baseplate. Why transparency is king.  
* **Imagery Needs:** Gallery of 4 compass types with "Pros/Cons" labels.

**Lesson 4.2: Anatomy of the Baseplate** (4 mins)

* **Content:** Labeling the Baseplate, Housing, Needle, and Index line.  
* **Imagery Needs:** Labeled high-res photo of a baseplate compass.

**Lesson 4.3: Setting the Map (Red to the Shed)** (6 mins)

* **Content:** Orienting map to North. Left on map \= Left on ground.  
* **Video Note:** POV video of a tutor rotating body/map until needle aligns with grid lines.

**Module 4 Quiz: The Navigator's Instrument**

* **Question 1 (Matching):** Label the parts of a compass image.  
* **Question 2 (Multiple Choice):** "Which part of the needle aligns with grid-north?" (The Red End).

### **Module 5: Planning & Strategy**

**Lesson 5.1: The 5 Ds of a "Leg"** (7 mins)

* **Content:** Destination, Distance, Direction, Description, Dangers.  
* **Imagery Needs:** Graphic showing a map "Leg" with labels for each D.

**Lesson 5.2: The Journey Workflow (Legs & Route Cards)** (8 mins)

* **Content:** Breaking walks into "chapters." Identifying decision points. The Route Card as a "Group Script."  
* **Imagery Needs:** 3km route divided by circles (Waypoints) and arrows (Legs).

**Lesson 5.3: Handrails & Catching Features** (5 mins)

* **Content:** Navigating by "Lines." Using major features (roads/rivers) as a "Safety Net."  
* **Imagery Needs:** Map snippet highlighting a path leading to a river.

**Module 5 Quiz: Mission Control**

* **Question 1 (Ordering):** Drag the "5 Ds" into logical planning order.  
* **Question 2 (Image Answering):** "What Catching Feature stops you if you miss the junction?"

*Requirement: 80% to pass and unlock Silver Access.*

**Scenario 1: The Forest Trail Journey (Lowland/Linear)**

* **Part 1 (Grid Reference):** "You find an injured walker near a ruined building in the forest. Identify the 6-figure grid reference for the rescue team."  
* **Part 2 (Planning):** "Looking at your route card, you have a 1.2km leg along a path. If you are walking at 4km/h, how long should this leg take?"  
* **Part 3 (Handrails):** "The path follows a stream. What is the technical name for using this stream to guide you?" (Correct: Handrail).

**Scenario 2: The Farm & Hill Walk (Open/Rural)**

* **Part 1 (Map Orientation):** Video quiz: "You come to a T-junction at a farm. Based on the field boundaries and the farm buildings shown in the photo, is your map oriented correctly? (Yes/No)."  
* **Part 2 (Catching Features):** "You are heading toward a church. If you reach a major A-road, have you gone too far? What is this road called in navigation terms?" (Correct: Catching Feature).  
* **Part 3 (Contour Shapes):** "The path climbs steeply toward a viewpoint. Identify if the landform you are standing on is a Spur or a Valley based on the provided map snippet."

**Lesson 7.1: The Small Stuff (Knolls & Re-entrants)** (7 mins)

* **Content:** Understanding "Shoulders." Following ridge lines to stay on high ground.  
* **Imagery Needs:** Top-down map vs Perspective photo with features highlighted.
